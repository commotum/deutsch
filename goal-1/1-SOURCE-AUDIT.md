## Updated Assumptions

- The paper's bit convention is opposite the most common quantum-computing label convention: from (4), bit value 1 is the `+1` eigenspace of `q_z`, while bit value 0 is the `-1` eigenspace. This convention plausibly explains the control projectors and minus signs in (15)–(16), but does not verify them.
- Descriptors are introduced as global `2^n × 2^n` matrices, so tensor notation such as (15) is ambiguous unless local matrices and already-embedded global operators are distinguished.
- The source uses `U† A U` for Heisenberg evolution and `U† |z;t⟩` for the evolving eigenbasis. Multiplication and circuit chronology must be tested together.
- Parameter occurrence in a descriptor is, at most, a representation- and history-sensitive descriptor-dependence notion. It is not by itself operational information location.
- A global unitary can move a pure state vector into a fixed reference vector by conjugating observables. The analogous mixed-state statement needs purification, an enlarged system, or a different representation because unitary conjugation preserves rank and spectrum.
- Circuit approximation of a general dynamics does not by itself prove an exact continuum locality theorem. Later locality claims must name supported gates, Hamiltonians, channels, topology, and exact/approximate scope.
- Decoherence claims require a named channel, stable basis, environment state, and discarded subsystem. They are not consequences of the word “decoherence.”
- The Bell discussion requires a common probability space for counterfactual outcomes, independence assumptions, and almost-sure rather than unqualified pointwise equalities. A finite setting set is sufficient for the intended contradiction.
- Equation (45) in the original PDF is false at Boolean values `a_0=1`, `a_1=0`, `a_2=1`, where its two sides are 1 and 2. The canonical Markdown supplies the grouping already indicated by the next line, giving the valid complementary partition `a_0 = a_0 (a_1 ∨ a_2) + a_0 (1-a_1)(1-a_2)`. Stage 11 independently compiled both the historical counterexample and the valid partition, while its production Bell inequality did not depend on either identity.
